1. Introduction

Electrolyte analyzers are indispensable diagnostic instruments in modern medicine, designed to measure the concentration of vital ions in biological fluids such as whole blood, serum, plasma, and urine.

These devices serve as the cornerstone of metabolic monitoring, providing clinicians with real-time data essential for managing patient homeostasis.

Core Objective

Precision Diagnostics

Systems are engineered for extreme precision, often achieving a coefficient of variation (CV) of less than 1%. Their primary role is to assess acid-base balance and renal function.
